Physical Artifact — Jesse

Soldier Portrait, Medal Case & Deployment Letter

01

Overview

This artifact consists of a soldier portrait displayed within a medal case, accompanied by a deployment letter. It represents Kolin’s identity as a soldier through the lens of military recognition, formality, and honor. The artifact present Kolin in a controlled and idealized way framing him through symbols of achievement and duty. It act as a physical entry point into Kolin’s perspective, introducing the audience to his role before they engage with the more immersive parts of the narrative.

Soldier portrait, medal case, and deployment letter

02

Intent

The goal of this artifact was to create a ceremonial object that frames Kolin as a recognized war veteran. I wanted users to encounter him through the symbols that defined him at first such as medals for his act in war, a soldier’s portrait, and official documentation.

This artifact positions Kolin as a decorated soldier and sets the expectations of the users before the audience enters the VR experience and sees a more complex reality. At the same time, the artifact functions as a point of transition to guide users from the physical world in the VR experience.

Intent development image

03

Process

To build this artifact I combined two picture frames using a hinge to create a medal case that could open and present its contents in a more formal display like way. This helped established the object as something ceremonial instead of decorative.

On one side I attached thrifted medals to represent Kolin’s achievements. Using real materials was important because it added a sense of authenticity and the artifact feel more grounded and believable.

On the other side, I created a portrait of Kolin in uniform. After my initial sketch of the portrait, user testing said that the image felt too bright and lacked the emotional weight of our narrative. To address this, I darkened the portrait to give a more somber tone to better align with our narrative.

After establishing the visual elements, I focused on crafting a way to connect the artifacts to the VR experience. To do this, I created a deployment letter designed to feel like an official military document with an official Mariscal insignia. The letter serves a subtle instruction for users to pick up the VR equipment, framing it as if they were receiving military gear. This allowed for the transition in the VR experience to feel integrated in the narrative rather than separate from it.

04

Reflection

This artifact helped me understand how physical objects can shape a user’s perception before they enter a digital experience. Since I was not responsible for building the VR environment itself, this forced me to think about how to design for something adjacent to my work, creating a bridge instead of just a standalone artifact.

This reinforced the idea that storytelling does not just begin inside an experience, but also before it as well. By framing Kolin through a formal and controlled presentation, this artifact sets expectations that are later challenged once the user enters the VR world.
